Overview




The workflow contains the following steps: The client application sends information to the Amazon Fraud Detector endpoint. Amazon Fraud Detector predicts a risk score (in the range of 0–1,000) on the input data with an ML model that is trained using historical data. A score of 0 indicates that the prediction is considered to have the lowest possible risk, and a score of 1,000 indicates that the prediction is considered to have the highest possible risk. If the risk score for a particular prediction falls beneath a predefined threshold, there is no further action. If the risk score exceeds the predefined threshold (for example, a score of 900), the Amazon A2I loop starts automatically and sends predictions for human review to an Amazon A2I private workforce. The Amazon A2I interface will review the case, and make an adjudication (approve, deny, or send it for further verification). The approval or rejection results from the private workforce are stored in Amazon Simple Storage Service (Amazon S3). From Amazon S3, it can be directly sent to the client application.

Advantages:

1. You pay only for what you use, you avoid large upfront expenses.

2. Identify suspicious online payments Reduce online payment fraud by flagging suspicious online payment transactions before processing payments and fulfilling orders.

3. Detect new account fraud Accurately distinguish between legitimate and high-risk account registrations so you can selectively introduce additional checks—such as phone or email verification.
