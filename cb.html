Conclusion & Bibilography

In this work, we propose a GNN-based imbalanced learning method named PC-GNN to solve the class imbalance problem in graphbased fraud detection. The overall framework could be decoupled into three steps: Pick, Choose, and Aggregate. The center nodes are picked with a label-balanced sampler to construct a balanced sub-graph for mini-batch training. Under a parameterized distance function, the neighborhood of the minority class is over-sampled and that of the majority class is under-sampled. Messages from selected neighbors and different relations are aggregated to obtain the final representations of the target. Experiments on two benchmark opinion fraud datasets and two real-world financial fraud datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed framework. For future work, besides label-aware graph sampling methods, learning a new graph structure for the imbalanced graph could be a promising direction for graph-based fraud detection..
